The Investigation
Chapter 01 · Google Sheets

Side Hustle Opportunity Scorecard

Which online business models survive a data-driven stress test?

Google Sheets SUMPRODUCT VLOOKUP INDEX/MATCH Conditional Formatting What-If Analysis

I scored 29 online business models across 9 criteria using a weighted SUMPRODUCT formula — giving higher weight to saturation resistance and passive income potential over surface-level metrics. The result: a live, interactive scorecard that reranks models as you adjust your priorities, built for the South African market context.

Key Findings
  • Tech & SaaS leads all categories — No-code Apps and AI Video Editing score 7.4/10
  • Dropshipping scores only 4.5/10 — USD exposure kills ZA viability
  • Passive income models score 1.2 points higher on average than active ones
  • Streaming is the worst investment at 3.9/10 — avoid entirely

Chapter 02 · SQL + Python

Digital Marketing Autopsy

Which campaign channels actually convert — and where is budget being wasted?

Python SQLite Pandas Seaborn Matplotlib Google Colab

I pulled 20,000 records from a 200K-row Kaggle dataset into a SQLite database, ran 5 business-focused SQL queries, then moved to Python for a 4-chart EDA. The notebook reads like an analyst memo — hypothesis, query, visualisation, recommendation — not a code dump.

20K
Records
Analysed
Key Findings
  • Influencer campaigns have the highest conversion rate across all types
  • YouTube delivers the best ROI — long-form content compounds over time
  • Health & Wellness and Tech Enthusiasts are the most valuable segments
  • Email and Display have the lowest return — budget reallocation recommended

Chapter 03 · Machine Learning · Live App

WillItWork? — Success Predictor

A deployed ML app that tells you if your side hustle will be profitable within 6 months.

Python Scikit-learn Random Forest Streamlit Joblib GitHub Pages

I generated a 1,500-record training dataset using viability scores from Project 1 as base success probabilities — directly connecting all three projects. The Random Forest model is trained, evaluated, and deployed as a live Streamlit app. Enter your business details and get a prediction with personalised recommendations.

Project Connections
Project 1 → Project 3: Viability scores from the Scorecard were used as base success probabilities to generate the ML training dataset. The projects are not isolated — they share data.
Project 2 → Project 1: The marketing channel analysis in Chapter 2 validates the platform recommendations made in the Scorecard's What-If tool.
Project 3 → All: The WillItWork app references Project 1 scores directly in the UI — users can look up their niche saturation score from the Scorecard and plug it into the predictor.
The Limitation: ML training data is synthetic. A production version would use survey data from real ZA entrepreneurs — an honest gap documented in every README.
